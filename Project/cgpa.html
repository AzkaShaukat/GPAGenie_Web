Understanding CGPA

The Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) is a measure of a student's academic performance across all semesters or academic periods completed. Unlike SGPA, which reflects performance in a single semester, CGPA provides an overall average, giving a broader picture of consistency and achievement over time. It is calculated by taking the sum of the grade points earned in all courses across all semesters and dividing it by the total number of credit hours attempted in those semesters. The CGPA is often used by universities, potential employers, and for academic honors.

How CGPA is Calculated:

CGPA = Σ (SGPAi × Creditsi) / Σ Creditsi

Where 'i' represents each semester.

Note: This calculator assumes you are entering the final SGPA and total credits earned for each completed semester. Different institutions may have slightly different methods for calculating CGPA, especially regarding repeated courses or transfer credits. Always refer to your institution's official guidelines.

Tips for Improving Your CGPA

Focus on Current Semesters

The most effective way to boost your CGPA is to perform well in your current and upcoming semesters, can significantly average up your cumulative score.

Address Weaknesses

Analyze which subjects or types of courses have been challenging in the past. Seek help from professors, tutors, or study groups in those areas.

Prioritize High-Credit Courses

Courses with more credit hours have a larger impact on your GPA. Focus extra effort on these subjects to maximize their positive contribution.

Develop Study Habits

Consistent studying, effective time management, and active engagement in classes are fundamental to improving performance in any semester.
