Understanding GPA & Grades

Grade point average (GPA) is a commonly used indicator of an individual's academic achievement in school. It is the average of the grades attained in each course, taking course credit into consideration. Grading systems vary in different countries, or even schools.

Letter Grades and Numerical Equivalents

This calculator accepts letter grades and translates them into numerical values as shown below. Grades like P (pass), NP (not pass), I (incomplete), or W (withdrawal) will be ignored in the calculation (select "Ignore").

  • A+ = 4.3 grade points
  • A = 4.0 grade points
  • A- = 3.7 grade points
  • B+ = 3.3 grade points
  • B = 3.0 grade points
  • B- = 2.7 grade points
  • C+ = 2.3 grade points
  • C = 2.0 grade points
  • C- = 1.7 grade points
  • D+ = 1.3 grade points
  • D = 1.0 grade point
  • D- = 0.7 grade points
  • F = 0.0 grade points

Note: Most schools, colleges, and universities in the United States use a grading system based on the letters above, though E is sometimes used instead of F. Grading systems do differ however based on what constitutes an A or B, and some do not include grades such as an A+ or a B-. Others may attribute more weight to certain courses, and thus whatever grade is attained in the course will have a larger effect on overall GPA. The calculator can account for this based on the number of credits attributed to a course, where credit is the "weighting" of the course, as shown in the examples below.

Examples of GPA Calculation

Example 1:

Course Credit Score Grade Points
Math 4 A+ 4 x 4.3 = 17.2
Physics 2 B 2 x 3.0 = 6.0
English 3 A 3 x 4.0 = 12.0
Total 9 NA 35.2
GPA 35.2 / 9 = 3.91

Example 2:

Course Credit Score Grade Points
Biology 4 A- 4 x 3.7 = 14.8
Chemistry 3 C 3 x 2.0 = 6.0
Chemistry Lab 2 B+ 2 x 3.3 = 6.6
Total 9 NA 27.4
GPA 27.4 / 9 = 3.04

Tips for Improving Your GPA

Attend All Classes

Presence is crucial. You'll absorb more information and understand expectations better.

Active Participation

Engage in discussions, ask questions, and contribute. It reinforces learning and shows initiative.

Time Management

Use planners or apps to schedule study time, assignments, and breaks. Avoid procrastination.

Effective Study Habits

Find what works for you – flashcards, study groups, summarizing notes, practice problems. Focus on understanding, not just memorization.

Utilize Resources

Visit professor's office hours, use tutoring centers, consult library resources, and collaborate with peers (appropriately).

Prioritize Assignments

Understand the weight of each assignment/exam. Allocate effort accordingly, focusing on high-impact tasks.

Review Feedback

Don't just look at the grade on returned work. Understand the feedback and learn from mistakes.

Plan Your Course Load

Balance challenging courses with manageable ones. Don't overload yourself, especially with subjects you find difficult.
